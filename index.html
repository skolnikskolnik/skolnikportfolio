About Julie Altman

Hi! My name is Julie Skolnik Altman, and welcome to my portfolio page! I'll tell you a little about me, my background, my interests, and how that influences my work.

I grew up in Cherry Hill, NJ and I went to Rutgers University, where I studied chemistry. After finishing my undergrad, I went to the University of Chicago, where I completed an MS in Chemistry. In graduate school, I taught organic chemistry and focused my own studies on Physical and Theoretical chemistry, which is where I had my first experiences with computer programming.

After finishing my BA and MS, I decided to become a chemistry teacher, and I have been teaching at the high school level for over seven years. In addition to teaching chemistry, I was also the coach of the Robotics team for two years. As the coach of the Robotics team, I had to learn how to work with Arduino hardware and software in order to teach my students how to make the devices they wanted to make. I thought this was going to be 20% teaching and 80% supervising, and to an extent that was true, but I was shocked at how much of my teaching was debugging my students' programs.

I was approached to bring AP Computer Science Principles to my school, despite having no formal background in computer science. I worked with code.org, both online and via in-person trainings, and I really love teaching AP CS Principles. The AP CSP course is geared towards young students, mostly freshmen, who have little to no background in computer science. I love how the curriculum is accessible and rigorous at the same time, and the lesson plans build conceptual understanding of key concepts. This isn't an ad for code.org! This is really how I feel about the curriculum.
